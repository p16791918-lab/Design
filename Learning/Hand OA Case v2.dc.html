No definite clinical synovitis → 2010 ACR/EULAR entry criterion not met. RF high but nonspecific; anti-CCP (−) and ESR/CRP normal.

FINAL DIAGNOSIS
Hand Osteoarthritis
Nodal / primary generalized OA — bilateral DIP + thumb IP (+ knee)

1990 ACR Criteria — Hand OA

Hand pain / stiffness + ≥3 of the following 4:
  • ✅ Hard-tissue enlargement of ≥2 of 10 selected joints
  • ✅ Hard-tissue enlargement of ≥2 DIP joints
  • ✅ Fewer than 3 swollen MCP joints
  • ✅ Deformity of ≥1 of 10 selected joints

Supporting Exam Findings

  • Heberden nodes at R 2nd/4th & L 2nd/5th DIP
  • Lateral deviation deformity at both 2nd DIP & R 4th DIP
  • No MCP swelling
  • → Hand OA criteria satisfied

Disease Review · Osteoarthritis
OA — Definition · Pathophysiology · Classification

Definition

Most common joint disease — progressive loss of articular cartilage with subchondral bone remodeling and osteophyte formation. Fundamentally a non-inflammatory, mechanically driven disorder.

Pathophysiology

  • Mechanical stress + biologic factors → chondrocyte imbalance
  • ↑ matrix metalloproteinases → cartilage matrix breakdown
  • Cartilage loss · subchondral sclerosis · osteophytes

Classification

TypeExamples
PrimaryLocalized (hand, knee, hip, spine) or generalized
SecondaryTrauma · metabolic · congenital · inflammatory

Hand OA subtypes

  • Nodal — Heberden (DIP) & Bouchard (PIP)
  • Thumb-base (1st CMC) OA
  • Erosive (inflammatory) OA
Risk: age · female · obesity · joint overuse / occupation · genetics.

Disease Review · Osteoarthritis
Hand OA — Diagnosis, the RF Pitfall & Management
"A positive RF is not a diagnosis."
Interpret it against anti-CCP, ESR/CRP, and the joint exam

Diagnosis

  • Clinical + radiographic: JSN, osteophytes, subchondral sclerosis, no erosion
  • 1990 ACR classification criteria for hand OA

The RF Pitfall

  • RF nonspecific — positive in healthy elderly, other autoimmune disease, chronic infection, HCV
  • Anti-CCP is the RA-specific antibody

Management (ACR / OARSI)

Non-pharmEducation · hand exercise · joint protection · thumb-base orthosis · heat
First-lineTopical NSAIDs
EscalationOral NSAIDs (lowest effective dose) · IA steroid for flares
Not indicatedDMARDs (no role in OA) · routine opioids
