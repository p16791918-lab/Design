Discussion & Key Takeaways
"A positive RF is not a diagnosis."
Interpret it against anti-CCP, ESR/CRP, and the joint exam

Why RF misleads

  • Not specific for RA — positive in other autoimmune disease, chronic infection, and healthy elderly
  • Anti-CCP is the specific antibody; pair with ESR/CRP + synovitis

Nodal Hand OA

  • Heberden nodes (DIP) · Bouchard nodes (PIP)
  • Bony enlargement — not soft-tissue synovitis

Take-Home Points

  • Separate OA from RA by joint pattern, stiffness duration, inflammatory markers, and erosions
  • Do not over-call RA from an isolated RF
  • Sjögren needs serology + objective sicca testing before diagnosis
OA has no disease-modifying drug — management is symptom control and joint protection.
References — Harrison's Principles of Internal Medicine, 22nd ed. (2024) · ACR Subcommittee on Osteoarthritis Guidelines, Arthritis Rheum 43(9):1905–15, 2000 · 1990 ACR & 2010 ACR/EULAR classification criteria.
